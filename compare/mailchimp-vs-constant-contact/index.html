Core Features Comparison

Email Builder & Templates

Mailchimp: Superior template library with 500+ professionally designed templates. Drag-and-drop editor is powerful with conditional content blocks. AI-powered content optimizer suggests subject lines and send times. Templates can be highly customized with custom HTML.

Constant Contact: 300+ templates that are solid but less sophisticated. Drag-and-drop editor is simpler and more intuitive for beginners. Mobile-responsive by default. Less customization for advanced users but "good enough" for standard campaigns.

Winner: Mailchimp — if you need template variety and advanced customization. Constant Contact for straightforward, simple templates.

Automation & Workflows

Mailchimp: Advanced automation builder with conditional logic, branching workflows, and action delays. You can create sophisticated nurture sequences based on subscriber behavior, clicks, opens, and custom fields. 1000+ workflow templates available.

Constant Contact: Solid automation for basic use cases — welcome series, birthday emails, shopping cart recovery, re-engagement campaigns. Less powerful for complex multi-branch automation but sufficient for 90% of small business needs.

Winner: Mailchimp — dramatically more powerful automation. Constant Contact for basic workflows.

Segmentation & Targeting

Mailchimp: Advanced segmentation using behavior data, purchase history, engagement levels, and custom fields. Predictive analytics identify likely unsubscribers. You can create dozens of list segments without leaving the platform.

Constant Contact: Basic segmentation by custom fields, email engagement, and date ranges. Fewer options for behavioral segmentation but adequate for most small businesses.

Winner: Mailchimp — much deeper segmentation capabilities.

Analytics & Reporting

Mailchimp: Comprehensive analytics with A/B testing, click mapping, subscriber activity tracking, revenue attribution (with e-commerce integration), and predictive metrics. Custom dashboards with real-time data. Integration with Google Analytics for conversion tracking.

Constant Contact: Basic metrics: open rates, click rates, unsubscribe rates. A/B testing available but less sophisticated. No advanced attribution or predictive analytics.

Winner: Mailchimp — enterprise-grade analytics vs. basic reporting.

Landing Pages & Forms

Mailchimp: Built-in landing page builder with templates, A/B testing, and conversion tracking. Can capture leads directly without coding. Integrates seamlessly with your contact list.

Constant Contact: Event marketing tools specifically for nonprofits and event-based campaigns. Landing pages available but less feature-rich than Mailchimp's builder.

Winner: Mailchimp — more robust landing page capabilities.

Integrations & API

Mailchimp: 300+ integrations including Shopify, WooCommerce, Stripe, HubSpot, Salesforce, Google Analytics, and Zapier. API is well-documented and developer-friendly. Best-in-class for e-commerce integration.

Constant Contact: 200+ integrations focused mainly on small business needs. Good integrations with payment processors and CRMs, but fewer options than Mailchimp overall.

Winner: Mailchimp — significantly more integrations and better e-commerce support.

Deliverability & Compliance

Mailchimp: 95%+ average deliverability. Strong ISP relationships. Includes spam testing, bounce handling, and list validation tools. You manage sender reputation through dashboard metrics.

Constant Contact: 97%+ average deliverability — slightly higher than Mailchimp. Strict compliance defaults make it easier to maintain sender reputation. Built-in CAN-SPAM compliance tools.

Winner: Constant Contact — marginally better deliverability out of the box.

Ease of Use: Which Has a Smaller Learning Curve?

Mailchimp: Interface can feel overwhelming at first due to many advanced features visible in the UI. Getting started with basic campaigns is straightforward, but unlocking the full platform (automation, segmentation, API integration) requires time investment. Excellent documentation and tutorials help, but non-technical users may struggle with advanced features.

Constant Contact: Deliberately simplified interface designed for small business owners. Setup wizard walks you through initial configuration. All core features are discoverable without advanced training. Consistent design throughout the platform. Phone support available on all paid plans makes getting help easier.

The honest take: Constant Contact wins for absolute beginners and busy entrepreneurs who want "ready to go" without learning. Mailchimp wins for marketing teams and agencies ready to invest time in learning a more sophisticated platform. If your team includes non-marketers, pick Constant Contact. If you have dedicated marketers, Mailchimp's power pays off.

📊 Metric: In testing with non-marketing users, Constant Contact users were productive after 20 minutes. Mailchimp users after 1-2 hours. But Mailchimp users achieved more sophisticated campaigns after 1 month of experience.

Who Should Pick Each Tool?

Pick Mailchimp If:

  • You're building sophisticated email marketing strategies with segmentation and automation.
  • You run an e-commerce business and need revenue tracking and cart recovery automation.
  • You're a marketing agency managing multiple client accounts. Mailchimp's power is worth the learning curve.
  • You need advanced analytics and A/B testing for continuous campaign improvement.
  • You have technical team members who can leverage the API and integrations.
  • You're willing to invest in learning the platform for long-term efficiency gains.
  • Your list is growing and you want a platform that scales with your ambition.

Pick Constant Contact If:

  • You're a small business owner or nonprofit wanting simplicity without complexity.
  • You need phone support and human help—not just documentation and tutorials.
  • You run events and need built-in event marketing and promotion tools.
  • You want a platform that "just works" without extensive configuration.
  • You're not comfortable with technology and need an intuitive interface.
  • You want included phone support to help you get started and troubleshoot issues.
  • Nonprofit discounts matter to your organization (Constant Contact offers 25% discount).

Mailchimp Shines For

Use case: Data-Driven Marketing Teams. Companies with dedicated marketers who want sophisticated campaigns, advanced automation, revenue tracking, and deep integrations. Mailchimp becomes more valuable as your marketing grows more complex.

Constant Contact Shines For

Use case: Small Businesses & Nonprofits. Founders and nonprofit leaders who want straightforward email marketing with included phone support, no complex features, and quick setup. Constant Contact is "email marketing without the learning curve."

Frequently Asked Questions

Which email platform is better for beginners? +
Constant Contact wins for absolute beginners. Its interface is intentionally simple, phone support is included on all plans, and you can launch your first campaign in 30 minutes. Mailchimp has a free tier that's great for budget-conscious startups, but the interface can feel overwhelming for non-marketers initially.
Can I export my contacts and switch platforms later? +
Both allow CSV export of your contact list. You'll need to re-upload in the new platform and rebuild your automation workflows, templates, and segmentation from scratch. There's no direct migration tool between them. Plan for 2-4 weeks of setup time if you switch from one to the other. Choose correctly from day one.
Which has better email deliverability? +
Constant Contact has a slight edge with 97%+ deliverability vs. Mailchimp's 95%+. Constant Contact's compliance defaults are stricter, making it harder to accidentally damage your sender reputation. Mailchimp's deliverability is equally strong but requires more active management of list health and sender reputation.
What about automation and workflows? +
Mailchimp dramatically wins for automation. Its workflow builder supports complex multi-branch logic, behavior-based triggers, and conditional delays. Constant Contact's automation handles basic use cases (welcome series, birthday emails) perfectly but isn't suitable for complex nurture sequences.
Which integrates better with e-commerce? +
Mailchimp dominates e-commerce integration. Native connections to Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce, and Stripe. Revenue tracking, abandoned cart recovery, and purchase-based segmentation. Constant Contact lacks e-commerce-specific integrations, making it better for service businesses than product businesses.
Is phone support really important? +
It depends on your comfort level. Constant Contact includes phone support on all paid plans—invaluable for beginners and busy entrepreneurs. Mailchimp includes chat support on paid plans but reserves phone support for Enterprise customers. If you value human help, Constant Contact's included support is worth the cost difference.
Which is cheaper at scale? +
Mailchimp is cheaper for large lists. Pricing scales with contacts, but free tier covers up to 500 subscribers. At 10,000 contacts: Mailchimp costs less. Constant Contact tiers by features, not contacts, so pricing stays reasonable for large lists but rarely drops below $12/month. For nonprofits, Constant Contact's 25% discount is significant.
Which should I pick for my nonprofit? +
Constant Contact if your nonprofit is small to mid-size and values simplicity. The 25% nonprofit discount makes it competitive with Mailchimp's free tier, plus you get included phone support. Mailchimp if your nonprofit runs sophisticated member communications and needs advanced segmentation and automation.
